## The Data

The database now contains 47 member-reported compound protocols. For this analysis, the most comprehensively tracked case was selected as the index: a treatment-resistant depression case with 24 months of monthly blood panels, SHAPS scoring every 2 weeks, and biomarker tracking across 47 markers. Six standard antidepressants had been trialed and failed before any of the compounds below were introduced. This is a single-case deep analysis — not a controlled trial. The value is in the granularity of the data, not the sample size.

The SHAPS (Snaith-Hamilton Pleasure Scale, range 0-56, clinical threshold >29, remission <7) was selected as the primary outcome measure because it is validated, specific to anhedonia, and insensitive to placebo effects from nonspecific mood improvement. Depression scales that mix mood and somatic symptoms are confounded by interventions that affect sleep or energy without affecting the core pathology.

## Analysis

### Why Cerebrolysin Produced the Largest Effect

Cerebrolysin is fundamentally different from standard antidepressants in its mechanism. Standard antidepressants modulate neurotransmitter levels (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine). The implicit model is that depression is a neurotransmitter deficiency — a model with modest effect sizes (SSRI standardized mean difference vs placebo: approximately 0.3) and high failure rates (STAR*D trial: 33% never achieved remission despite aggressive sequenced treatment).[^1]

Cerebrolysin delivers neurotrophic factors directly: BDNF, NGF, GDNF, CNTF, and others. The implicit model is that depression involves structural brain changes — reduced hippocampal volume, decreased synaptic density, impaired neurogenesis — and that recovery requires reversing these changes, not adjusting the chemical soup. This model also explains the timeline: Cerebrolysin produced noticeable effects within 1-2 weeks, while antidepressants require 4-6 weeks for receptor adaptations.

[^1]: STAR*D (Sequenced Treatment Alternatives to Relieve Depression), *NEJM* 2006. Largest real-world depression treatment study. Remission rate after first medication: 37%. Cumulative remission after four treatment steps: 67%.

The BDNF increase in this case (+90%, from 18.2 to 34.6 ng/mL) is consistent with Cerebrolysin's mechanism. A 2021 meta-analysis in *Frontiers in Psychiatry* (DOI: 10.3389/fpsyt.2021.665274) found significant antidepressant effects for Cerebrolysin but noted limited evidence base due to small sample sizes. The Research Collective database provides granular single-case data that complements the published literature.

### Why the Combination Mattered

No single compound produced remission. BPC-157 addressed inflammation (HS-CRP: -87%) and dopamine function. Semax boosted BDNF through a different pathway. Cerebrolysin delivered the largest neurotrophic push. Magnesium threonate provided maintenance. The combination was more effective than any single compound — a finding consistent with the database's broader pattern that multimodal protocols outperform single-compound interventions.

## Implications

1. **Treatment-resistant depression requires mechanistic targeting, not algorithmic medication trials.** The standard algorithm (SSRI → SNRI → augmentation → MAOI) failed six times in this case. Compounds selected for mechanistic fit to the biomarker profile succeeded.

2. **Biomarker tracking is not optional.** Without SHAPS scoring, BDNF measurement, and HS-CRP tracking, the effects of each compound would have been indistinguishable from placebo, mood fluctuation, or coincidence. Objective tracking made the protocol iterable.

3. **The database compounds in value with each new protocol report.** This single-case analysis is informative. The aggregate analysis across 47+ members — identifying which biomarker profiles respond to which interventions — is what makes the Research Collective's data irreplaceable.

## Methodology

Index case: treatment-resistant depression, 24 months of tracked self-experimentation. SHAPS administered every 2 weeks. Blood panels monthly (47 markers).
