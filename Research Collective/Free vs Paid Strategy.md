# Free vs Paid Strategy — The Research Collective

## The Core Principle

> Free content proves you have the data and the methodology. Paid content is the database they can't get anywhere else.

The free reader should finish every article thinking: *"This person actually tracks biomarkers, analyzes real data, and knows the science. If the free articles are this data-rich, the full database must be incredible."*

## What Goes Where

### Always Free
Content that:
- Establishes scientific credibility and data-driven approach
- Is shareable (counterintuitive findings, biomarker revelations)
- Drives email capture
- Has broad appeal across compounds and conditions

**Examples:**
- "The 12 Blood Markers Your Doctor Won't Order (And Why They Matter)"
- "What 3 Years of Monthly Blood Panels Actually Shows"
- "BPC-157 and Dopamine: What the 2025 Kyoto Study Actually Means"
- "I Analyzed 200 Self-Experimenter Blood Panels. Here's What I Found."
- "The Pre-Protocol Checklist I Learned the Hard Way"
- Compound comparison deep-dives with aggregate data

### Always Paid
Content that:
- Is the actual protocol database — compound, dose, timing, biomarkers to track
- Contains verified sourcing intelligence (vendors change constantly)
- Is the crowd-sourced bloodwork comparison database
- Would cost thousands to replicate independently (lab testing, sourcing research)
- Has network effects — more valuable with more members contributing

**Examples:**
- "The Complete BPC-157 Protocol Guide" — dose schedules, biomarker tracking, expected timeline
- "Verified Vendor Report: June 2026" — batch tests, COAs, shipping intel
- "Member Blood Panel Deep-Dive" — real data, anonymized, patterns extracted
- "Compound Interaction Matrix" — what stacks safely with what
- "Group Buy: Third-Party Testing Pool" — members pool funds for lab verification
- "Personal Protocol Design" — 1-on-1 consultation

## The Bridge Content

Articles that sit between free and paid, showing the depth available behind the paywall:

| Free Article (shows the pattern) | Paid Content (delivers the full dataset) |
|---|---|
| "The 3 markers most self-experimenters ignore" | Full 47-marker tracking guide with optimal ranges |
| "BPC-157: intranasal vs subcutaneous — one finding" | Complete 47-person comparison dataset |
| "One compound that fixed my anhedonia" | Full protocol library organized by condition |
| "Why I test monthly" | Member bloodwork database with 200+ panels |

## The Paywall Moment

The subscriber doesn't pay for content. They pay for:

1. **Safety** — One bad sourcing decision costs thousands in medical bills. The community catches errors before they happen.
2. **Efficiency** — Stop guessing. Protocols backed by real bloodwork data instead of forum anecdotes.
3. **Access** — Sourcing intelligence that isn't on Google. Vendor verification. Group buys.
4. **Community** — Self-experimenting alone is isolating and dangerous. Second eyes on your protocol. Crowd-reviewed bloodwork.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "$149/month is expensive" | "One specialist visit is $300-500. One bad compound batch could cost you your health. This is insurance for your self-experimentation." |
| "I can find this on Reddit for free" | "Reddit is where people post anecdotes. We post bloodwork. The free forums are entertainment. This is infrastructure." |
| "I'm not running a complex protocol" | "Every self-experimenter starts simple. The database grows with you. And the harm reduction content alone is worth it." |
