## Why This Works

Grey-market self-experimentation is:
- **Desperate** — people with treatment-resistant depression, anhedonia, chronic fatigue who have exhausted all mainstream options
- **High-spending** — already dropping $200-500/mo on compounds; $149/mo for safety intel is a rounding error
- **Information-starved** — the real knowledge is scattered across dying forums, private Discords, and Telegram groups
- **Dangerous when done alone** — bad sourcing can literally kill you; community verification saves lives
- **Long-term** — people self-experiment for years; they need ongoing sourcing updates (vendors change constantly)

The content bridges the information gap. Free content proves the methodology (bloodwork, rigor, data). Paid content delivers the database.

## Persona

**Dr. Elias Voss** — Biochemist by training. Self-experimenter by necessity. 6 years in academic biochemistry, 8 years of self-experimentation. Fixed his own treatment-resistant depression. Reversed his cognitive decline. Now runs monthly blood panels, tracks 47 biomarkers, and shares everything openly.

Not a medical doctor. Does not give medical advice. Shares data — bloodwork, protocols, mistakes, and what actually moved the needle.

**Voice:** Direct, clinical but warm. Uses "I" statements. Cites specific biomarkers and numbers. Self-deprecating about past failures. References published papers casually.

**Signature phrase:** "The data doesn't lie, but it also doesn't tell the whole story."
