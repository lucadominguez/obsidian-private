TOPIC 1: BPC-157 (Angiogenesis, Dopamine Modulation, Wound Healing)
=================================================================

Paper 1: PMID 21030672 | DOI: 10.1152/japplphysiol.00945.2010
  Chang CH et al. J Appl Physiol. 2011;110(3):774-780.
  BPC 157 accelerates tendon fibroblast outgrowth, increases cell survival
  under oxidative stress, and promotes migration via FAK/paxillin pathways.
  Figures: YES (outgrowth images, dose-response, migration assays)

Paper 2: PMID 27138887 | DOI: 10.2174/1570159x13666160502153022
  Sikiric P et al. Curr Neuropharmacol. 2016;14(8):857-865.
  BPC 157 modulates dopamine system (counteracts receptor blockade,
  amphetamine effects, haloperidol catalepsy). Demonstrates brain-gut axis.
  Figures: YES (signaling schematics, dose-response data)

Paper 3: PMID 29998800 | DOI: 10.2174/1381612824666180712110447
  Seiwerth S et al. Curr Pharm Des. 2018;24(18):1972-1989.
  BPC 157 promotes angiogenesis via VEGFR2 and VEGF-A pathway. Matches or
  exceeds standard growth factors (EGF, FGF, VEGF). NO system interaction.
  Figures: YES (angiogenic pathway diagrams, tissue histology)

TOPIC 2: ME/CFS Biomarkers (NK Cells, EBV, Tilt Table/POTS)
=================================================================

Paper 1: PMID 39483457 | DOI: 10.3389/fimmu.2024.1440643 (OPEN ACCESS)
  Baraniuk JN et al. Front Immunol. 2024;15:1440643.
  Meta-analysis of 28 papers, 55 data points. NK cytotoxicity reduced to
  ~HALF of healthy controls. Most consistent immune finding in ME/CFS.
  Figures: YES (forest plots, funnel plots, effect size distributions)

Paper 2: PMID 33114612 | DOI: 10.3390/biom10111484 (OPEN ACCESS)
  Kerr JR. Biomolecules. 2020;10(11):1484.
  EBI2 and EGR genes 1/2/3 upregulated in EBV-associated ME/CFS.
  Mechanism: stress -> weakened immunity -> EBV reactivation -> EGR cascade.
  Figures: YES (gene expression heatmaps, pathway diagrams, clustering)

Paper 3: PMID 40627388 | DOI: 10.1073/pnas.2422546122 (OPEN ACCESS)
  Eckey M et al. PNAS. 2025;122(5):e2422546122.
  Survey of 3,925 ME/CFS + Long COVID patients. POTS/orthostatic intolerance
  in >60%. Beta blockers, ivabradine most effective for autonomic symptoms.
  Figures: YES (treatment heatmaps, cohort visualizations)

TOPIC 3: Homocysteine + Cognitive Decline (MTHFR, Methylation)
=================================================================

Paper 1: PMID 26820674 | DOI: 10.1007/s12035-016-9722-8
  Rai V. Mol Neurobiol. 2017;54(2):1173-1186.
  Meta-analysis of 41 studies. MTHFR TT genotype: AD risk OR ~1.3 overall,
  ~1.5 in Asians. Hcy >14 μmol/L = ~2x AD risk.
  Figures: YES (forest plots, ethnic-stratified OR plots)

Paper 2: PMID 25449138 | DOI: 10.1016/j.ejmg.2014.10.004
  Liew SC, Gupta ED. Eur J Med Genet. 2015;58(1):1-10.
  MTHFR C677T: ~15% TT in Caucasians, ~5% Africans, ~20% some Asians.
  TT = thermolabile enzyme (70% reduced activity). Hcy >15 μmol/L.
  Figures: YES (global allele maps, folate cycle schematic)

Paper 3: PMID 34432056 | DOI: 10.1093/nutrit/nuab057
  Wang Z et al. Nutr Rev. 2022;80(4):931-949.
  Systematic review/meta-analysis of B vitamin RCTs. Hcy reduced 3-5 μmol/L.
  Slower brain atrophy (0.5%/year) in VITACOG trial (Hcy >11 μmol/L).
  Figures: YES (forest plots, brain atrophy comparisons)

TOPIC 4: HS-CRP + Cardiovascular Risk (Reynolds vs. Framingham)
=================================================================

Paper 1: PMID 17299196 | DOI: 10.1001/jama.297.6.611 (LANDMARK)
  Ridker PM et al. JAMA. 2007;297(6):611-619.
  24,558 women, 10.2-year follow-up. Assessed 35 factors. hs-CRP = strongest
  novel predictor. Reynolds reclassified 40-50% of intermediate-risk women.
  Figures: YES (calibration plots, reclassification tables, ROC curves)

Paper 2: PMID 22399535 | DOI: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.111.075929
  Cook NR et al. Circulation. 2012;125(14):1748-1756.
  Direct Framingham vs. Reynolds comparison. 1722 CVD cases. NRI = +4.9%
  (p=0.03). Better calibration (H-L p=0.30 vs. 0.001). Better stroke
  prediction (c-stat 0.806 vs. 0.778). Driven by hs-CRP + family history.
  Figures: YES (calibration plots, reclassification tables, ROC comparison)

Paper 3: PMID 33292676 | DOI: 10.1186/s40959-020-00078-2 (OPEN ACCESS)
  Lee K et al. Cardiooncology. 2020;6:27.
  100 breast cancer survivors, 16-week exercise RCT. RRS reduced from 3.0%
  to 1.8% (p<0.001). hs-CRP fell 0.8 mg/L (p=0.002). RRS clinically useful.
  Figures: YES (RRS waterfall plots, hs-CRP change by group)

TOPIC 5: Low-Dose Naltrexone (LDN) - Mechanism & Trials
=================================================================

Paper 1: PMID 30248938 | DOI: 10.3390/medsci6040082 (OPEN ACCESS)
  Toljan K, Vrooman B. Med Sci (Basel). 2018;6(4):82.
  LDN = 1-5 mg/day. DUAL MECHANISM: (1) Transient opioid blockade ->
  endorphin/enkephalin upregulation -> OGF-OGFr signaling. (2) TLR4
  antagonism on microglia -> reduced TNF-a, IL-1b, IL-6.
  Figures: YES (mechanism diagrams, dose comparison chart)

Paper 2: PMID 29377216 | DOI: 10.1002/phar.2086
  Patten DK et al. Pharmacotherapy. 2018;38(3):382-389.
  Systematic review: Fibromyalgia pain -28.8% (57% had >30% reduction vs
  21% placebo). MS: SF-36 +9 points. Crohn: 67% response, 33% remission.
  Cost ~5-40/month.
  Figures: YES (forest plots, bar charts, mechanism schematic)

Paper 3: PMID 38267326 | DOI: 10.1016/j.clinthera.2023.12.009
  Tamariz L et al. Clin Ther. 2024;46(3):e101-e106.
  Post-COVID cohort. LDN vs amitriptyline/duloxetine. Fatigue HR 2.3
  (p=0.001), brain fog HR 2.1, pain HR 1.9. 8 weeks to improvement vs 14+.
  Figures: YES (Kaplan-Meier curves, HR forest plots)
