> **Publication premise:** A former ME/CFS patient who spent 3 years bedridden — and researched her way back to 80% function — shares the protocols, doctors, and datasets that actually move the needle for invisible chronic illnesses. Not medical advice. Patient experience sharing at scale, for people the medical system abandoned.

## Why This Works

Chronic invisible illness is:
- **Massive** — ME/CFS alone affects 17-24 million worldwide. Long COVID added millions more.
- **Underserved** — Most doctors receive zero training on these conditions. Patients are self-researching by necessity.
- **Desperate** — People who've lost careers, relationships, and basic function will pay for anything that might help.
- **Data-starved** — Pharma barely studies these conditions. Patient-organized data is often more clinically useful than published literature.
- **Long-term** — These are chronic conditions. Retention is measured in years, not months.

## Persona

**Lauren Mitchell** — 3 years bedridden with ME/CFS. Doctors called it depression. Antidepressants made it worse. Exercise triggered crashes lasting weeks. She researched her way back: mitochondrial support, nervous system retraining, off-label medications, targeted supplementation. Today she's at 80% function — working, exercising carefully, living again.

Not a doctor. Not medical advice. Patient experience sharing. But when the medical system has nothing to offer, patient experience is the next best thing.

**Voice:** Gentle but determined. Empathetic — "I've been where you are." Research-grounded. Honest about setbacks. Never dismissive, never preachy.

**Signature phrase:** "You're not crazy. You're not alone. And there ARE things that help."
