# Free vs Paid Strategy — Chronic Illness Hacking

## The Core Principle

> Free content proves you've walked the path. Paid content is the map.

The free reader should finish every article thinking: *"She actually understands what this is like. If she recovered even partially, I need to know exactly what she did."*

## What Goes Where

### Always Free

- Personal recovery story and timeline
- Lab testing guides (which tests matter for which conditions)
- Medical gaslighting awareness (universal, highly shareable)
- Single protocol teasers with broad applicability
- General pacing and lifestyle strategies
- Doctor-finding advice (general tips)

### Always Paid

- Full condition-specific protocol guides with dosing
- Doctor recommendation database (names, locations, specialties)
- Lab result crowd-review service
- Personalized protocol design
- Supplement and medication experience database
- Private condition-specific support channels

## The Paywall Moment

Subscribers pay for:

1. **Hope with evidence** — Not "you might get better" but "here's what 200 people with your condition tried and what happened"
2. **Doctor access** — Names and locations of doctors who actually listen
3. **Protocol specifics** — Exact compounds, doses, timelines from real patient experiences
4. **Community** — People who understand. No explaining. No justifying.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "$99/month is a lot when I can't work" | "I was on disability when I started. The information here saved me years of trial and error. One good doctor recommendation is worth years of suffering." |
| "I can find this on patient forums for free" | "Patient forums are fragmented, unverified, and full of dangerous advice. We organize, verify, and track outcomes." |
| "Nothing works for me anyway" | "That's what I thought too. Sometimes it's not the protocol — it's the doctor, the dose, or the timing. We help you find what you're missing." |
