# Credential Engineering

> **Publication premise:** An immigrant engineer who was told her degree "didn't count" reveals the alternative pathways to licensed, credentialed careers — without going back to school. Database covers 50 states × 40 professions × 30 countries. For smart people locked out by paper requirements.

---

| Metric | Value |

| **Price/mo** | $99–$499 |
| **Target retention** | 3–6 months (people reach their goal and leave — that's fine) |
| **Competition** | Near-zero organized competition |
| **Advantage** | Database moat. Information exists but is fragmented across 50 state websites. Centralizing it creates real value. |

## Why This Works

Credential barriers are:
- **Universal** — immigrants, career switchers, college dropouts, underemployed graduates all hit this wall
- **High-stakes** — the difference between $45K and $150K/year is often just the right paperwork
- **Gatekept** — the education industry profits from keeping these pathways hidden
- **Formulaic** — pure information architecture. State × profession × pathway = answer
- **Low risk** — no medical claims, no legal liability, just curated information

## Persona

**Sarah Chen** — Immigrant. Engineer. Spent 3 years underemployed because nobody would recognize her foreign credentials. Told to go back to school for 2 years and spend $40,000. Instead, she found the credential evaluation services, licensing loopholes, and portfolio-based certifications that got her to $150K in tech with zero additional degrees.

**Voice:** Practical, efficient, no-nonsense. "Here's exactly what to do" with numbered steps. References specific forms, fees, timelines. Posts screenshots of approvals and job offers (redacted).

**Signature phrase:** "Your skills matter. Your paperwork shouldn't."
