## Day 2 — Value Post (Tom)

**DENIAL LETTER DECODED: "Your claim does not meet the criteria for coverage."**

I've written this exact sentence hundreds of times. Here's what it actually means:

**Translation:** "We found a reason — any reason — that could arguably exclude coverage. We're hoping you'll accept this and go away."

**The fix:** Ask for the SPECIFIC policy language they're citing. Not "the policy says." The exact section, paragraph, and sentence. Then read that language. 60% of the time, it doesn't actually say what they claim it says.

**Second fix:** Ask for the specific facts that support their determination. "What evidence did you rely on?" Make them commit to a position in writing.

When they have to cite specific language and specific facts, their position often falls apart. That's why they use vague language like "does not meet criteria." They're hoping you won't push.

Push.

## Day 3 — Struggle Post + Owner Response

**Amplifier member:** "My roof claim got denied for 'pre-existing damage.' The adjuster spent 5 minutes on my property and never even went on the roof. How do I fight this?"

**Tom:** Classic. They always cite pre-existing when they didn't actually inspect. Here's your play:

1. Demand a re-inspection in writing. Specify: "I am requesting a re-inspection by a different adjuster. The original inspection did not include a roof-level evaluation."

2. Get your own contractor assessment. Three quotes minimum. Document everything with photos.

3. File a complaint with your state's Department of Insurance. Use the phrase "bad faith claims handling." Those words trigger regulatory attention.

## Day 7 — Value Post: The Adjuster's Playbook (Tom)

**THE ADJUSTER'S PLAYBOOK: 7 tactics they use on every claim**

1. **Delay** — The longer it takes, the more desperate you get. Desperate people settle for less.
2. **Lowball first offer** — They expect you to negotiate. First number is always 40-60% of actual value.
3. **Vague denials** — "Does not meet criteria" instead of specific policy language. Harder to fight.
4. **Request unnecessary documentation** — Make you do work. Hope you give up.
5. **Good cop / bad cop** — Supervisor is always "unavailable" when you want to escalate.
6. **Recorded statements** — They're not for your benefit. Every word can be used against you.
7. **Deadline pressure** — "This offer expires in 7 days." It never actually does.

Knowing these is half the battle. Next post: how to counter each one.
