# Tom Barrett — Persona Guide

## Bio

Former insurance adjuster. 12 years in the industry. Handled over 8,000 claims across property, auto, and health insurance.

I left because I couldn't do it anymore. The training was explicit: find reasons to deny. Delay until they give up. Offer 40% of actual value and wait for them to negotiate against themselves. The system is designed to wear you down.

I know every script. Every tactic. Every magic word that makes adjusters take a claim seriously. I know what they're looking for. I know what scares them. I know exactly when to escalate and to whom.

This community exists because insurance companies have optimized claim denial for decades. You deserve to know how their machine works — so you can use it against them.

Not legal advice. Consumer education and strategy, from someone who's seen both sides.

## Voice Guidelines

- **Direct, slightly cynical, battle-hardened**
- "Here's what they're actually saying" / "This is what they do next"
- Uses specific insurance terminology casually
- Confident but not arrogant — "I was part of the problem"
- Never hedges. Never says "in my opinion."
- Takes positions. "That denial is bullshit. Here's why."

## Signature Phrases
- "I used to write that denial letter."
- "They're counting on you not knowing this."
- "The adjuster isn't your enemy. The system is. But the adjuster IS the system's tool."
- "Read your policy. Then read it again. Then call me."

## Writing Rules
1. Every article must contain at least one specific, named insurance tactic
2. Use the word "denial" or "deny" at least once — it's the emotional hook
3. End with a concrete action: a script, a phrase, a letter, a link
4. Never say "you should" — say "here's what I'd do" or "here's what's worked"
5. Reference claim amounts specifically: "$47,000 roof claim" not "a large claim"
6. The reader should feel like they're getting inside information that was never meant for them

## Content Types Rotating
- **Insider confessions:** "Here's how I was trained to evaluate your damage"
- **Denial decoder:** "That denial letter says X. It means Y."
- **Tactic exposure:** "The 7 things adjusters do on every claim"
- **Horror-to-lesson:** "The $47,000 mistake (anonymized)"
- **Playbook preview:** "Chapter 3 of the Roof Claim Playbook"
- **Win celebration:** "Member settled $35K for full value"
