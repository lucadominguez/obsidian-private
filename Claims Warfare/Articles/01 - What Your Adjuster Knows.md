## The Seven Tactics

| # | Tactic | Mechanism | Countermeasure |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Delay | Longer claims = more desperate policyholders | Set written deadlines. File DOI complaint if missed |
| 2 | Lowball first offer | 40-60% of reserve. Anchors negotiation low | Reject entirely. Counter with contractor estimate |
| 3 | Vague denials | "Does not meet criteria" — no specific language cited | Demand exact policy section, paragraph, sentence |
| 4 | Document flood | Request unnecessary paperwork until you give up | Ask which provision requires each document |
| 5 | Good cop / bad cop | Supervisor "unavailable" for escalation | Request name + direct contact in writing |
| 6 | Recorded statement | Every word parsed for ammunition | Decline. Optional in most cases |
| 7 | Deadline pressure | "Offer expires in 7 days" — never does | Ignore. Get estimates. Take your time |

## Analysis

Adjuster performance is measured on three metrics: cycle time (speed of closure), severity (average payout per claim type — lower is better), and file quality (audit survival). Policyholder satisfaction is not a metric. Accurate valuation is not a metric. Fairness is not a metric. The incentive structure rewards adjusters who close claims quickly at the lowest number the policyholder will accept.

The data confirms the incentive effect: first offers average 40-60% of actual damages, and internal carrier studies (referenced in training materials) show that the majority of unrepresented policyholders accept without challenge. The system is not broken. It is optimized for a different objective than the policyholder assumes.

---

## Implications

1. **Document everything as if building a court case.** Photos of damage AND undamaged areas. Written log of every interaction. Contractor estimates before the adjuster's estimate.

2. **Never accept the first offer.** The first number is an anchor, not a valuation. Counter with documentation.

3. **Escalate systematically.** Every "final offer" is final only for the person making it. Supervisor authority limits are higher. VP authority is essentially unlimited.

4. **The appraisal clause is the most underused tool in claims.** It bypasses the adjuster entirely and produces binding results. Adjusters count on policyholders not knowing it exists.
