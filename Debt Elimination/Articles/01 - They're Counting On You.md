## Summary

Consumer debt is bought and sold on a secondary market at 4-7 cents on the dollar. The documentation degrades with each sale. Federal law (FDCPA §1692g) gives debtors the right to demand proof. This briefing documents the weapons available, their success rates, and the order in which to deploy them.

---

## The Weapons — In Order

| Priority | Weapon | When | Cost | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1st | Statute of Limitations | Debt 3-6+ years old | $0 | 100% elimination (legally dead) |
| 2nd | Debt Validation Letter | Within 30 days of collector contact | ~$5 | ~70% success (can't produce docs) |
| 3rd | Settlement Negotiation | Debt is valid & collectible | 25-40% of balance | 60-75% savings |
| 4th | Credit Report Dispute | Negative items on report | ~$5/letter | 40+ pts per removal |
| 5th | Pay-for-Delete | Verified debt, clean report | Negotiated | Complete removal |

## Analysis

The debt collection industry operates on information asymmetry. Debtors don't know that their $10,000 debt was purchased for approximately $500. They don't know that 70% of collectors can't produce the documentation required by FDCPA §1692g. They don't know that making a single payment on a time-barred debt restarts the statute of limitations clock.

The weapons are sequential. Statute of limitations is the first filter — if the debt is legally dead, nothing else matters. Validation letters are the second filter — if the collector can't prove they own the debt, they can't collect. Settlement is the third option — for debts that are valid and collectible. Credit report disputes and pay-for-delete are the cleanup phase.

---

## Implications

1. **Never pay without validation.** The FDCPA gives you 30 days from first contact to demand proof. Use it.
2. **Never pay a time-barred debt.** Even $1 restarts the statute of limitations clock.
3. **Never give electronic account access to a debt collector.** Cashier's check only.
4. **Get every agreement in writing.** Verbal settlement offers are unenforceable.
5. **The system is designed for compliance, not fairness.** The rules exist. They favor the party that knows them.
