QUESTION 1: IS IT COLLECTIBLE?
Is the debt past the statute of limitations in your state?
YES
→ IGNORE THE DEBT
Legally uncollectible. They cannot sue. Do not pay a cent — even $1 restarts the clock. Do not acknowledge in writing.
SOL: 3-6 years depending on state & type
NO / UNSURE
→ SEND VALIDATION LETTER
FDCPA §1692g. Certified mail, return receipt. They MUST stop all collection until they prove you owe it.
Success rate: ~70% can't produce docs
QUESTION 2: CAN THEY PROVE IT?
Did they validate with complete documentation?
NO (70% OF CASES)
→ DEBT IS DEAD
Cannot collect. Cannot report. Dispute with all 3 credit bureaus — they have 30 days to verify or remove. If they can't verify, it comes off your report permanently.
Eliminated this way: $22,000 across 7 accounts
YES — THEY HAVE PROOF
→ NEGOTIATE SETTLEMENT
Stop paying 4-6 months for charge-off. Counter their 60-70% offer with 25-30%. Get agreement IN WRITING on letterhead. Pay by cashier's check only — never give electronic account access.
Best settlement: $14K → $4,200 (30%)
QUESTION 3: IS YOUR REPORT CLEAN?
Is the debt still on your credit report after resolution?
YES — DISPUTE OR PAY-FOR-DELETE
→ CREDIT REPORT WARFARE
Dispute letters to Equifax, Experian, TransUnion. Certified mail. If can't verify in 30 days → removed. For verified debts: negotiate pay-for-delete — payment in exchange for complete removal.
Items removed: 7 · Score jump: ~40 pts each

→ REBUILD CREDIT
