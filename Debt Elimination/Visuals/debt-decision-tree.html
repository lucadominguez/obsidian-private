Question 1
Is the debt past the statute of limitations in your state?
YES
→ IGNORE THE DEBT
The debt is legally uncollectible. They cannot sue. Do not pay anything — even $1 restarts the clock. Do not acknowledge the debt in writing.
Statute of limitations: 3-6 years depending on state & debt type
NO (or unsure)
→ SEND VALIDATION LETTER
Federal law (FDCPA) gives you 30 days to demand proof. Certified mail. Return receipt. They MUST stop all collection activity until they provide proof.
Success rate: ~70% can't produce documentation
Question 2
Did they validate the debt with complete documentation?
NO
→ DEBT IS DEAD
They can't collect. They can't report it. Dispute it with credit bureaus — if they can't verify within 30 days, it gets removed.
Average debt eliminated this way: $22,000 across 7 accounts
YES — THEY HAVE PROOF
→ NEGOTIATE SETTLEMENT
Stop paying. Wait 4-6 months for charge-off. Counter their first offer (60-70%) with 25-30%. Get the agreement IN WRITING before paying. Use cashier's check — never give electronic account access.
Best settlement: $14K → $4,200 (30%)
Question 3
Is the debt still on your credit report after resolution?
YES — DISPUTE OR PAY-FOR-DELETE
→ SEND CREDIT REPORT DISPUTES
Send dispute letters to all 3 bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). If they can't verify within 30 days, the item is removed. For verified debts: negotiate pay-for-delete — trade payment for complete removal.
Items removed from my report: 7 · Score jump per removal: ~40 pts
